## Hi there 👋

Computer Science student at the University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee focused on software engineering, human-computer interaction, and AI systems design. I build human-centered technology that combines scalable engineering with behavioral and cognitive research.

My technical work centers on Java, Python, C++, object-oriented design, data structures, algorithms, and AI engineering foundations. I enjoy building modular, interactive systems that prioritize usability, accessibility, and thoughtful user experience.

I’ve developed projects ranging from Java-based social experience platforms and BST-backed appointment management systems to inventory management software and driver’s exam evaluation applications. My work has included modular architecture design, recursive data structure implementation, event-driven programming, user input systems, testing/debugging, and building scalable object-oriented applications using Java, JavaFX, Git, and GitHub.

Beyond software engineering, my research experience explores the intersection of AI, ethics, and human behavior. I’ve conducted qualitative and technical research on AI in healthcare and social media misinformation, combining stakeholder interviews, behavioral analysis, and technical evaluation to study how technology shapes trust, decision-making, and human interaction.
